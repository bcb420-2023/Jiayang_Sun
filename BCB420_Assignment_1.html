Filter out low count genes according to edgeR:

# Load the expression data and convert it to a matrix of raw counts
counts <- as.matrix(read.table(fnames[1], header = TRUE, row.names = 1, sep = "\t"))
# Create a DGEList object:
dge <- DGEList(counts = counts, genes = rownames(counts))
# Filter out genes that have less than 3 samples with a count-per-million (cpm) of at least 1:
keep <- rowSums(cpm(dge) >= 1) >= 3
dge_filt <- dge[keep,]

Filter out duplicate genes:

# Identify duplicated gene names:
dup_genes <- duplicated(rownames(dge_filt))
# Subset dge_filt to exclude duplicated genes:
dge_filt_unique <- dge_filt[!dup_genes,]

  • What are the control and test conditions of the dataset? The dataset “GSE189820” contains gene expression data from duodenal biopsy samples of three groups: healthy controls, CVID enteropathy patients without villous atrophy (CVID noVA), and CVID enteropathy patients with villous atrophy (CVID VA). The comparisons are between CVID noVA vs healthy controls and CVID VA vs healthy controls, as well as between CVID noVA vs CVID VA. Therefore, the control conditions are the healthy controls, and the test conditions are CVID noVA and CVID VA.

Common variable immunodeficiency (CVID) is an immune system disorder, and typically, immune system disorders are usually of unknown cause and difficult to cure. The results of this analysis can provide insights into the molecular basis of CVID enteropathy and potentially identify biomarkers for diagnosis and treatment.
